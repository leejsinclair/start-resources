Getting up to speed

Customers

It's important for fledging businesses to focus their efforts on products and services that have potential to either earn $ or build a customer base for the purpose of pursuing funding.

  • Know your customers
  • Know how to reach them
  • Determine which product/service has the greatest chance of success,
  • Determine a business model that supports a sustainable business,

Hard facts

  • Incubators are looking for between 5-7% growth per week

A company making $1000 a month (a typical number early in YC) and growing at 1% a week will 4 years later be making $7900 a month, which is less than a good programmer makes in salary in Silicon Valley. A startup that grows at 5% a week will in 4 years be making $25 million a month. [1]

  • Our calculations put 5% growth at 4 years at 5% should result in $8,214,906 per week.

[1] What if a company grew at 1.7x a year for a really long time? Could it not grow just as big as any successful startup? In principle yes, of course. If our hypothetical company making $1000 a month grew at 1% a week for 19 years, it would grow as big as a company growing at 5% a week for 4 years. But while such trajectories may be common in, say, real estate development, you don't see them much in the technology business. In technology, companies that grow slowly tend not to grow as big.
